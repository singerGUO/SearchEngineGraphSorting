The Facts (Seattle)

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation, search

The Facts Newspaper /Seattle Facts is an African-American weekly newspaper that serves Seattle, Washington. Headquartered in the Central District, it was founded in 1961 by Fitzgerald Redd Beaver. His children, Marla, LaVonne Beaver are currently the publishers and editors [1]The Facts is a member of the West Coast Black Publishers Association and bills itself as "The Voice of the Northwest African American Community."[citation needed]

The longtime headquarters of The Facts prior to November 2009.

After being located at the corner of Cherry Street and Martin Luther King, Jr. Way (originally Empire Way) since the 1960s, The Facts moved to 1112 34th Avenue on November 23, 2009.[2]
