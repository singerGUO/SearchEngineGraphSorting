Steven Randall "Randy" Jackson (born October 29, 1961) is an American singer-songwriter, musician and dancer. Jackson is best known as a former member of his family band, The Jacksons. Jackson was the youngest son and the second youngest Jackson sibling before his sister Janet Jackson was born.[2]

Early life[edit]

From left, back row: Jackie Jackson, Michael Jackson, Tito Jackson, Marlon Jackson. Middle row: Randy Jackson, La Toya Jackson, Rebbie Jackson. Front row: Janet Jackson, 1977.

Randy was born in Gary, Indiana to Joseph Jackson and Katherine Jackson. Nicknamed "Little Randy", Jackson is the ninth child of the Jackson family and youngest of the Jackson brothers.[2] Jackson was only three years old when the Jackson 5 was formed and therefore was not an original member. While his brothers toured, Jackson honed his skills as a musician, mastering piano and the bongos.

Career[edit]

The Jacksons[edit]

Randy was not an original member of The Jackson 5, first officially appearing live with his brothers at the end of 1971.[3] The event was a Christmas show the Jackson 5 held for blind children.[4] Although he was on every Jackson 5 tour from 1972 forward mainly playing the congas among other instruments, Randy did not officially join the family band until 1975 when they left Motown Records for Epic Records and older brother Jermaine chose to stay with Motown, prompting Randy to replace him.[2] The Jackson 5 officially changed their name to The Jacksons when they signed with Epic due in part to the fact Motown owned the name The Jackson 5.[3] At the age of 16, he co-wrote The Jacksons' most successful single on Epic, "Shake Your Body (Down to the Ground)" with Michael.[5]

Jackson plays congas, percussion, keyboards, piano, bass, and guitar among other instruments. In addition to singing and playing on the Jacksons' recordings, he worked with Michael on his Off the Wall album.[6] Jackson was able to fully participate in the Jacksons' 1981 Triumph Tour and in the band's later projects. He was part of the Jacksons 2001 reunion at Madison Square Garden, but did not appear as an official cast member in their 2009 A&E reality series The Jacksons: A Family Dynasty.[7][8] However, he did contribute backing vocals with Jackie, Tito and Jermaine for Michael's This Is It.

After The Jacksons[edit]

After recording the 2300 Jackson Street album, the group disbanded and focused on separate projects in 1990.[2][3] After this split, Jackson formed his own band, Randy & the Gypsys.[2] The group only released one album before breaking up.[9] On June 28, 1998, Jackson opened up his own record label Modern Records.[10]

Personal life[edit]

On February 4, 1980, Jackson was seriously injured in a car crash in Hollywood, California. In June 1980, Jackson appeared on the cover of the weekly African-American newsmagazine Jet. The cover headline read: "Randy Jackson Walks Again: Talks About His Future."[11] In 1986, Jackson met Alejandra Oaziaza. They dated for years but never married. Jackson and Oaziaza have two children together: Genevieve Jackson (b. December 3, 1989) and Steven Randall Jackson Jr. (b. October 2, 1991). Alejandra married and later divorced Randy's older brother, Jermaine Jackson. [2]

Battery charge[edit]

Later, Jackson married Eliza Shaffe in August 1989. Jackson and Shaffe have a daughter together, Stevanna Jackson (b. 1990).[2] In January 1991, Jackson was charged with beating his wife, Eliza Sahffe as well as their 14-month old daughter, Stevanna.[12][13] Jackson pleaded no contest to the battery charge.[12] In November that year, Jackson was arrested after Shaffe phoned the police to report that the beatings had not stopped.[14] Jackson was sentenced to one month in jail, two years probation, and ordered to enroll in a domestic violence rehabilitation program.[12][13][1] The couple divorced in 1992.

Child support[edit]

In January 2012, Jackson filed a complaint against Alejandra Oaziaza, disputing an alleged $500,000 owed in child support.[15][16] Jackson protested that he had never been served for the initial paternity lawsuit, instead claiming that the matter had been settled in 1993.[15] His absence had resulted in a default judgement in favor of Oaziaza.[15]

Bankruptcy[edit]

Jackson filed for bankruptcy in 1996.[17] Jackson was ordered to surrender his shares in Modern Records Inc.[18] In May 1998, an arrest warrant was issued when Jackson failed to turn over the stock. In 2001, Randy pleaded guilty to bankruptcy fraud by failing to list all his vehicular assets.[19]

Shooting[edit]

Michael Jackson's former bodyguard, Matt Fiddes, claimed that he had been ordered to shoot Randy by the deceased singer.[20] Randy denied these claims, pointing to other sensationalist claims made by Fiddes.[21]

Disputed will and child custody[edit]

Randy attempted to unseat executors John Branca and John McClain and dismiss his brother Michael's will, which reportedly made his mother Katherine furious.[22] He and his siblings Jermaine and Janet objected to the 2011 tribute concert held for Michael, stating that they felt the family's attention should have been focused on the trial of Conrad Murray.[23]

The three siblings were later involved in an incident where Katharine Jackson lost custody of Michael's children due to suspicion that she was "prevented from acting as a guardian because of the acts of third parties".[24] Randy, Jermaine and Janet attempted to take the children with them to Arizona, where it was alleged that Katherine Jackson was being held against her will after being kidnapped.[25] Footage of the incident appeared to show the siblings storming the house and Janet Jackson trying to take Paris Jackson's phone.[25][24] Sheriff's deputies broke up an altercation between Randy and Trent Jackson.[24] Eventually Katharine regained custody of Prince, Paris and Blanket.[26][27]
